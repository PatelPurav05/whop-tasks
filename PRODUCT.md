# Whop Tasks

## Register

product

## Users

Whop Tasks serves two participants who may use the same account in either workspace:

- Earners browse funded campaigns, reserve a limited slot, provide structured proof, respond to review feedback, and track simulated earnings.
- Businesses create multi-slot campaigns, define proof requirements, reserve demo funds in escrow, review submissions, and approve or reject work.

The primary context is operational. Users are moving quickly between discovery, evidence submission, review, and money states on desktop or mobile. The interface must keep ownership, deadlines, capacity, funding, and next actions visible.

## Product Purpose

Whop Tasks demonstrates one trustworthy, linked marketplace transaction from campaign funding through payout. Success means two authenticated accounts can complete the full lifecycle while every capacity decision, review transition, and simulated balance change is enforced on the server and explained in the product.

The product is a local-first interview build. Payments, withdrawals, KYC, ratings, chat, admin tools, and email are intentionally outside this phase. In-app notifications and an immutable demo ledger are included because they make the core transaction understandable.

## Brand Personality

Immediate, intentional, and confident. Copy uses clear active verbs, frames outcomes positively, and combines urgency with effortless grace. Whop's values of resolve, agency, grit, and excellence should be visible in decisive workflows and practical feedback, never in ornamental slogans.

Approved action language includes: `Claim task`, `Submit proof`, `Review submission`, `Approve and pay`, `Request changes`, and `Get paid`.

## Anti-references

- A marketing splash page where the marketplace should be.
- A generic gig board with anonymous listings, weak funding context, or unclear next steps.
- A speculative crypto or fintech aesthetic with neon-on-black styling.
- Decorative gradients, glass effects, nested card grids, side-stripe accents, gradient text, and gratuitous motion.
- Get-rich-quick imagery, vague claims, fake controls, or broad generalizations.
- Social templates, milestone graphics, or pictograms repurposed as routine product chrome.
- Any redrawn, recolored, distorted, shadowed, or approximated Whop logo.

## Design Principles

1. **Make the transaction legible.** Show funding, remaining capacity, ownership, deadlines, status, and the next valid action where decisions happen.
2. **Earn trust through invariants.** Never imply success before the server confirms it. Make escrow, payouts, refunds, expiry, and stale-review behavior explicit.
3. **Keep work moving.** Use short operational flows, direct verbs, generated proof fields, useful defaults, and recoverable errors.
4. **Use familiarity as an affordance.** Follow Whop and Frosted UI conventions for navigation, forms, status, progress, and feedback.
5. **Reserve delight for earned moments.** Official pictograms may support discovery, meaningful empty states, and payout confirmation, while everyday actions stay quiet and precise.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA. All actions must be keyboard reachable, expose visible focus, preserve semantic labels and state announcements, meet contrast requirements, and use touch targets suitable for mobile. Never communicate status by color alone. Respect reduced-motion preferences and keep state transitions between 150 and 250 milliseconds. Support light and dark themes without changing task meaning or logo legibility.

## Confirmed Scope

The accepted implementation plan is the confirmed shape brief. The current phase establishes the official brand source, application foundation, authentication, database model, migrations, and domain services. Public, earner, and business interfaces remain out of scope beyond the minimum application shell needed to prove the foundation.
